Panettone

Description

Panettone is a very commom sweet bread of Italian origin. It's also consummed during Christmas all over the world including other countries in South America, USA and, of course, Europe. There are many stories about the creation of panettone. According to historians, it's a collective recipe so the origin is not certain. But the panettone we all know today was created in Milan, Italy, by Angelo Motta.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg of sifted flour
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • A pintch of salt
  • 1 table spoons of butter
  • 250 ml of warm milk
  • 50g of dry active yeast
  • 300g of candied fruits
  • 250g of raisings
  • 20 ml of panettone flavor extract (optional)
  • Lemon zest

Steps

  1. Blend the eggs, sugar, butter, milk, yeast and panettone extract for 30 seconds.
  2. In a big bowl, put the sifted flour and make a hole in the middle. Pour mixed ingredients into the hole and mix everything together.
  3. Mix the dough until it's not sticking anymore.
  4. Then, add the fruits, raisings and lemon zest.
  5. Put the dough in a baking pan and leave it until it doubles in size.
  6. Cut a X on top of the dough and brush beaten egg yolks.
  7. Bake until golden brown.
