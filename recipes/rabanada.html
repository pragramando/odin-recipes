Rabanada

Description

Rabanada is a sweet very common in Brazil during Christmas. There are many variations of this plate aroun the world, like french toast in the US and pain perdu in France. Even though people usually tend to think that rabanada comes from Portugal, it's probably from France or at least has French influence. Another interesting fact is that in the 15th century rabanadas used to be given to women after they gave birth.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of bread (you can use any kind of bread)
  • 2 cups of milk
  • ½ can of condensed milk
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ cup of vegetable oil (used to fry)
  • ½ cup of sugar
  • Cinnamon powder

Steps

  1. Cut the bread into slices.
  2. In a bowl, mix the milk with the condensed milk and put it aside.
  3. In another bowl, beat the eggs until they are very well combined.
  4. Heat up the vegetable oil in a pan.
  5. Mix sugar and cinammon powder together.
  6. Soak the bread slices in the milk mixture but get rid of any excess milk.
  7. Put the bread slices in the egg mixture and then take them out to fry in the pan. Fry each side for about 2 minutes or until they turn golden.
  8. Remove excess oil from fried bread.
  9. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar on top of the bread.
